About Me

I first started my undergraduate coursework at Hunter College as Psychology and biological sciences majors. I had immigrated to the United States the year before to pursue higher education and work opportunities that were not available to women in Iran. In my third year as a student there, I enrolled in a computer science class to satisfy my general education requirements and quickly became fascinated with the subject. I was drawn to how much I could relate my observations about the world around me with the concepts I studied in my textbooks. In that first class, I met the man who later became my husband. He is a very successful Software Engineer and works for a startup company. His special passion for Computer coding and computer science class excited and motivated me in both an intellectual and deeply personal way.

Coding gives me the power of creating solutions to problems and putting my creative ideas into action. It is amazing to see that the code you write, will develop in the live system and actual people will use it.
